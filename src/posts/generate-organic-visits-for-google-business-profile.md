## What Is a Google Business Profile?


Now, before we get into tips, let's agree on definitions.


When a person searches for either your business or a service you provide on Google or Google Maps, the free Google Business Profile (GBP) listing appears. Information about a business name, phone number, website address, photos, working hours, reviews, and much more is generally included.


For example, if someone types in "coffee place near me" and you have a small coffee shop in your locality, that profile can show up, really, particularly if it's optimized very well.


### Why Organic Visits Matter


In the terminology we use, organic visits refers to people finding your Google Business Profile and clicking on it without you paying for any ads. These visitors come via natural searches, maps, or suggestions from Google.


Organic visits matter for the following reasons:


- They are free.
- They are likely to be people actively searching for your products or services.
- They build trust and credibility.
- The increases in activities that happen on your profile will also increase the ranking of that profile by Google.


At [Hitman Digital](/), we always recommend focusing on organic performance first—because it’s cost-effective and long-term.


Now let's look at ways to improve those organic visits step-by-step.


### 1. Set up and verify your Google Business Profile


If you have not yet done or claimed your profile, this is the very first thing to do. Go to google.com/business to follow their process to verify ownership or management of the business. To verify, Google usually sends a postcard to your business address or uses another method.


This is a necessary step. Without verification, you cannot edit or optimize your listing.


### 2. Fill Out Every Section Completely


Google appreciates complete profiles, and potential customers will as well. Work in the following data:


- Your real business name
- A description of whatever it is you do in as much detail as you can possibly manage
- Latest business hours, including holiday hours
- Phone number
- Link to the website

Service areas, whether you are going to the customers or they are coming to you.
A natural use of keywords should be placed in your business description. Should you be in the bakery business, appropriate keywords may include "fresh pastries," "birthday cakes," or "custom cupcakes."


### 3. Add High-Quality Photos Regularly


Photos matter. Google says businesses with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks.


Here's what you can post:


- Exterior and interior photos of your business
- Images of products or services
- The team's or staff's image
- Behind-the-scenes images (baking, creating, serving, etc.)
- Customer images (with permission)

The more you keep adding fresh photos, the more Google will view your profile as an active one, which in turn leads to organic visits for the Google Business Profile.


### 4. Collect and Respond to Reviews
Reviews are something that packs a punch. They form trust and serve as influencers for potential customers on what to do.
The happy customers are the ones who should be encouraged to put in a review, including sending them a direct link for convenience.


Always reply to reviews—good or bad. Say thanks for the feedback and handle negative ones politely and professionally. Responding to them shows you that you mean business and makes your profile quite engaging.


### 5. Use Keywords in Your Business Description and Posts


Customer-focused. What would someone search for when looking for your business?


Assuming you are a plumber based in Dallas, a prospect would search for something like nullify.


- "Emergency plumber in Dallas"
- "Drain cleaning service near me"
- "Water heater repair Dallas."

Now, naturally embed those keywords within your descriptions and posts. Not so much stuff there, just make sense.
This is a primary tactic that generates organic visits for the profile on Google Business.


### 6. Post Weekly Updates


Google allows you to make regular posts on your profile, like mini-updates or announcements. It is like social media.


Here's what you can post:


- New products or services launched
- Limited-time offer
- Behind-the-scenes video
- Customer shout-out or review

**FAQs**


Images and a good call to action, such as 'Call now,' 'Visit our website,' or 'Learn more.'
It helps keep your profile current and visible more often in search. Consistent posting will ensure that it stays current and visible in most of the searches.


### 7. Enable Messaging


You can enable direct messaging on your profile using Google for people to contact you directly from their devices. It's a fast and easy way to reach more people, answer their questions, and turn visits into customers.
Remember to check regularly for messages because slow responses can adversely affect your profile's ranking.


### 8. Use the Q&A Section Wisely

In your form, one of the sections caters to public questioning on your business. You may also add common queries that you may answer.


Think of adding an FAQ section here. You can also include common questions people often ask, such as:


- “Do you give free estimates?”
- “Are you open on Sundays?”
- “Do you do deliveries?”


This is somewhat informative for searchers and gives a natural boost to your keywording through this."


### 9. Track Your Performance with Insights
Through Google Business Profile, one gets access to statistics on the performance of their listing. You get to know:

- The number of views for your profile
- The source through which customers found you (search or maps)
- The actions taken by the customers (calls, direction requests, website clicks)
- The most viewed pictures

Use this data to build your strategy. For instance, if a particular post performed well, do it again, but create something in a similar way.


### 10. Keep Your Info Up to Date

A simple thing, really—an important thing. Always keep all of your business info accurate, especially hours, services, and contact info. If there is a change, make sure you update it immediately.
See if a customer picks up on this inconsistency or outdated information, it ends up losing their trust. And Google has a weakness for profiles that are fresh and reliable.
